Bayesian Statistics


in the Era of Machine Learning

Project Discription

The last decade has seen tremendous progress on computational data analysis. New frameworks such as Tensorflow [1] and Pytorch [2] as well as new languages such as Julia [3] have revolutionized the fields of machine learning and statistics. Modern software can leverage hardware like GPUs to provide parallel computation on a ever larger scale while also supporting modern techniqus such as automatic differentiation. Probabilistic programming languages [e.g. 4, 5] make strong use of such techniques to build fast and sophisticated inference algorithms that make Bayesian inference easy to implement, even for laymen.

The core of these tools usually consist of inference techniques such as Markov chain Monte Carlo algorithms or variational inference techniques [6].

The goal in this project will be to initially learn about some modern inference algorithms. This may include Hamiltonian Monte Carlo, the No U-Turn Sampler (NUTS) [7], variational mean field approaches or other posterior approximations as well as an understanding of their strengths and weaknesses. With this you may then take the project in any direction you find interesting. This could include an empirical comparison of algorithms (comparing speed and/or accuracy) or to find interesting applications/datasets to showcase their performance.
